Governance Model

Decision rights, review cadence, escalation paths, and accountability structures for managing the AI and automation initiative portfolio.

Foundation

Governance Principles

⚡
Decisiveness
Governance structures are designed to make decisions, not defer them. Every scored item receives a decision at each governance meeting.
⚖️
Proportionality
Decision authority matches the scale and risk of the initiative. Routine approvals are streamlined; high-stakes decisions require full committee.
🎯
Accountability
Value commitments are tracked and reported. Sponsors are accountable for their initiatives — and consequences exist for persistent gaps.
🔍
Transparency
Portfolio status, decisions, and rationale are visible to key stakeholders. Scoring criteria are published and applied consistently.
🔄
Adaptability
Governance structures evolve as the program matures and the environment changes. Annual framework reviews keep the model calibrated.
🛡️
Safety First
The CMO retains veto authority on any initiative with direct clinical decision-making involvement, regardless of composite score.
Structure

Governance Committee

The primary decision-making body for the AI and automation portfolio. Quorum: 5 of 8 members — must include the Chair and at least one clinical representative.

Chair — CEO or CSO
Final approval authority; tie-breaking vote; portfolio strategy
Chief Medical Officer
Clinical safety and quality veto authority
Chief Operating Officer
Operational feasibility and capacity
Chief Financial Officer
Financial value, ROI threshold, budget approval
CIO / Chief Digital Officer
Technical feasibility and enterprise architecture
Chief People Officer
Workforce impact and change readiness
Chief Compliance Officer
Risk, regulatory, and AI ethics
VP Strategy / Innovation Lead
Portfolio balance and strategic coherence

Operating Committee (PMO)

Manages the day-to-day pipeline: intake coordination, evaluation logistics, and progress tracking.

Cadence

Review Cadence

Monthly Decision Meeting
Monthly
Review scored use cases and make prioritization decisions.

Agenda:

  • 1. Recap of prior month decisions (5 min)
  • 2. New submissions ready for decision (30 min)
  • 3. Deferred use cases for re-review (15 min)
  • 4. Portfolio capacity check (10 min)
  • 5. Active initiative escalations (15 min)
  • 6. Decisions recorded and communicated (5 min)
Quarterly Portfolio Review
Quarterly
Review overall portfolio health, value realization, and strategic alignment.

Agenda:

  • 1. Portfolio dashboard review — all active initiatives (20 min)
  • 2. Value realization report — initiatives ≥ 90 days (20 min)
  • 3. At-risk initiative review (20 min)
  • 4. Strategic alignment check (15 min)
  • 5. Framework health and process feedback (10 min)
  • 6. Upcoming pipeline preview (15 min)
90-Day Post-Launch Review
Per Initiative
Validate that the initiative is on track to deliver committed value.

Outputs:

  • Early outcome data vs. baseline
  • Revised value forecast if needed
  • Risk and blocker status update
  • Recommendation: continue / adjust / escalate
12-Month Benefits Realization
Per Initiative
Confirm that committed value has been realized and is sustained.

Outputs:

  • Final KPI performance vs. targets
  • Confirmed financial impact (Finance-validated)
  • Lessons learned documentation
  • Recommendation: sustain / expand / retire / case study
Reporting

Portfolio Status Definitions

Each active initiative carries a status that is reported at the monthly meeting and in the portfolio dashboard.

StatusDefinitionRequired Action
GreenOn track; value commitments being met; no significant blockersNone required
YellowMinor risk or delay; value still achievable with interventionSponsor provides mitigation plan within 2 weeks
RedSignificant risk to value, timeline, or scope; intervention requiredEscalate to Governance; action plan due in 1 week

Escalation

Escalation Paths

1

Tier 1 — Sponsor Resolution (SLA: 5 business days)

Resource contention within the initiative team · Minor scope clarification · Access or permission blockers within the sponsor's control

2

Tier 2 — Operating Committee (SLA: 10 business days)

Cross-initiative resource conflicts · Technical blockers requiring IT leadership · KPI baseline disputes · Vendor or contract issues

3

Tier 3 — Governance Committee (Next meeting or emergency session)

Budget changes > 20% of approved · Scope changes that materially alter value commitment · Patient safety or compliance concerns · Requests to pause/retire a Tier 1 initiative

!

Emergency Protocol (48-hour session)

For patient safety issues or critical compliance breaches, an emergency governance session may be called within 48 hours by the CMO, CCO, or CEO. PMO lead coordinates the session.
