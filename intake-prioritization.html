Evaluation Framework

Prioritization Criteria

Every submitted use case is evaluated against five criteria that together determine whether it advances to active development. These criteria feed directly into the value scoring model.

Criterion 1
Business Value Potential
Expected measurable impact across quality, productivity, cost savings, revenue uplift, and risk reduction. Scored across five weighted value levers (0–5 each).
Criterion 2
Operational Impact
Scale of process change, number of users affected, and breadth of workflow transformation — from targeted task automation to end-to-end process redesign.
Criterion 3
Feasibility
Assessed across three dimensions: data readiness (availability and quality), technical complexity (integration and build effort), and regulatory risk (GxP, validation requirements).
Criterion 4
Scalability
Potential to expand the solution across additional business units, geographies, or use cases — maximizing portfolio return on a single capability investment.
Criterion 5
Strategic Alignment
Fit with organizational AI priorities, executive sponsor engagement, and roadmap timing. Initiatives without active sponsorship are held until alignment is confirmed.
How the framework connects
Once submitted here, a use case moves to Value Scoring where each criterion is quantified into a composite score (0–100) that determines its priority tier. KPIs are then defined in the KPI Framework to establish measurable success targets. Finally, projected and actual values are tracked in the Pipeline & Realization dashboard — closing the loop from idea to measured impact.
