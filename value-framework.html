Value Framework

A shared language for defining, measuring, and communicating the impact of AI and automation initiatives across six value domains.

Structure

Framework Architecture

Value is organized into three levels: domains, categories, and individual KPIs. Every approved initiative must select KPIs from this hierarchy before launch.

Three-Level Hierarchy

6 Value Domains → 3–5 Categories per domain → 3–8 KPIs per category

Each KPI Requires:

  • Metric name and precise definition
  • Measurement approach (quantitative or qualitative)
  • Baseline — current-state measurement before launch
  • Target — expected outcome at defined timeframe
  • Timeframe — when the target should be reached
The Six Domains

Value Domains in Depth

🩺
Domain 1 — Quality & Speed

Captures improvements in clinical quality, patient safety, diagnostic accuracy, and the speed of care delivery and operational processes. Healthcare organizations are measured on quality outcomes and accreditation standards; AI that improves quality creates durable, compounding value.

Captures increases in net revenue through improved coding, denial prevention, capacity utilization, and new service opportunities. AI that prevents revenue leakage and enables better capacity utilization creates direct bottom-line impact.

Captures improvements in clinician and staff experience, including reduction in administrative burden and burnout risk. Clinician burnout and staff turnover are significant cost and quality risks — AI that reduces burden protects both human capital and care quality.

Measurement

How Value Is Measured

📐
Quantitative
Numerically measurable, attributable to the initiative. Examples: cost savings, FTE reduction, revenue increase, cycle time reduction, denial rate.
🎯
Qualitative
Observed through structured assessment, surveys, or expert judgment. Examples: staff satisfaction, patient trust, care culture, strategic positioning.
🔄
Both Required
Every initiative must define at least one quantitative KPI. High-priority initiatives should supplement with qualitative measures for a complete picture.
Measurement LevelDescriptionExamplesMinimum for
ActivityInputs and outputs of the AI solutionVolume processed, tasks automated, alerts generatedAll initiatives
OutcomeChanges in the metric the initiative is designed to improveDenial rate, documentation time, CAHPS scoreAll initiatives
ImpactBroader organizational or financial resultNet revenue, staff retention, patient satisfactionTier 1 & 2 initiatives

Attribution Approach

AI initiatives rarely operate in isolation. Use these conventions to maintain credibility in value reporting.

ScenarioAttribution Approach
AI is the sole change in the environmentFull attribution
AI is one of several concurrent changesProportional attribution agreed with sponsor before launch
AI operates in a redesigned processAttribute to the combined initiative; document AI's contribution separately
Improvement is observed but causality is uncertainReport as correlated improvement; note uncertainty explicitly
Value Sizing

Value Tier Classification

Use this matrix to estimate expected value before detailed analysis. Tier classifications inform prioritization weight in the scoring model.

TierAnnual Impact EstimateClassificationTypical Sponsor Level
Tier 1 — Transformational > $10M or organization-wide quality/experience shift Strategic initiative C-suite / Board
Tier 2 — Significant $2M – $10M or major department-level improvement Priority initiative VP / SVP
Tier 3 — Moderate $500K – $2M or measurable team-level improvement Standard initiative Director
Tier 4 — Foundational < $500K or enabling capability for future value Improvement project Manager
