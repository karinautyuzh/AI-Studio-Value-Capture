# Intake & Prioritization Framework

> A structured process for submitting, evaluating, and prioritizing AI and automation use cases in healthcare.

---

## Overview

The Intake & Prioritization Framework establishes a repeatable, transparent process for organizations to manage a pipeline of AI and automation opportunities. It ensures that:

- All use cases are evaluated against consistent criteria
- Prioritization decisions are data-driven and defensible
- Resources are allocated to the highest-value, highest-readiness opportunities
- Clinical, operational, financial, and strategic perspectives are all represented

---

## Framework Principles

1. **Consistency** — All submissions go through the same intake process regardless of origin
2. **Transparency** — Scoring criteria and weights are published and visible to all stakeholders
3. **Inclusivity** — Use cases can be submitted from any function: clinical, operational, finance, IT, or strategy
4. **Speed** — The process is designed to move quickly from submission to decision
5. **Value Orientation** — Every prioritized initiative must have a defined value hypothesis and measurable KPIs

---

## Process Overview

```
[Submission] → [Initial Screening] → [Full Evaluation] → [Prioritization Decision] → [Initiation] → [Value Tracking]
```

| Stage | Owner | Timeframe | Output |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1. Submission | Use case sponsor | Ongoing | Completed intake form |
| 2. Initial screening | Innovation/PMO team | 5 business days | Pass/Hold/Return decision |
| 3. Full evaluation | Cross-functional committee | 10 business days | Scored evaluation |
| 4. Prioritization decision | Governance committee | Monthly cadence | Approved/Deferred/Declined |
| 5. Initiation | Program manager + sponsor | Per roadmap | Charter and KPI baseline |
| 6. Value tracking | Analytics + sponsor | Ongoing | Dashboard + reporting |

---

## Stage 1: Submission

Any employee, leader, or external partner may submit a use case using the **[Intake Form Template](../templates/intake-form-template.md)**.

### Submission Requirements

A complete submission must include:

- [ ] Problem statement and current-state description
- [ ] Proposed AI/automation solution concept
- [ ] Affected stakeholder groups and estimated scale
- [ ] Initial hypothesis on value (cost, quality, experience, revenue, workforce, or risk)
- [ ] Sponsoring leader name and confirmation of support
- [ ] Data availability self-assessment
- [ ] Known technical or regulatory dependencies

Incomplete submissions will be returned to the sponsor within 3 business days with a checklist of missing items.

---

## Stage 2: Initial Screening

The Innovation/PMO team reviews each submission against a minimum viability threshold.

### Screening Criteria

| Criterion | Pass Threshold |
|---|---|
| Problem is clearly defined | Yes |
| Solution concept is plausible | Yes |
| Executive sponsor is identified | Yes |
| At least one measurable value hypothesis | Yes |
| Not a duplicate of an existing initiative | Confirmed |
| No active regulatory/legal blockers | Confirmed |

Submissions that pass screening advance to Full Evaluation. Those that do not pass are either:
- **Returned** to the sponsor for revision (with feedback), or
- **Held** in a backlog if promising but not yet ready

---

## Stage 3: Full Evaluation

A cross-functional evaluation committee scores each use case using the **[Scoring Model](scoring-model.md)** and the **[Prioritization Scorecard Template](../templates/prioritization-scorecard-template.md)**.

### Evaluation Committee Composition

| Role | Perspective Represented |
|---|---|
| Chief Medical Officer or designee | Clinical quality and patient safety |
| VP Operations or designee | Operational feasibility |
| Chief Financial Officer or designee | Financial value and budget |
| Chief Information/Digital Officer or designee | Technical feasibility and data readiness |
| Chief People Officer or designee | Workforce impact |
| Compliance/Legal representative | Risk and regulatory |
| Strategy/Innovation lead | Strategic alignment |

The committee may delegate scoring to subject matter experts but retains decision authority.

### Evaluation Inputs

For each use case, evaluators review:

1. Completed intake form
2. Any supplemental documentation from the sponsor
3. Comparable reference cases or benchmarks where available
4. IT/data assessment (requested in parallel with screening)
5. Compliance/regulatory assessment where applicable

---

## Stage 4: Prioritization Decision

The Governance Committee meets on a **monthly cadence** to review scored evaluations and make portfolio-level prioritization decisions.

### Decision Outcomes

| Outcome | Definition | Next Action |
|---|---|---|
| **Approved — Priority** | Score ≥ 75; proceed to initiation immediately | Assign PM, set charter timeline |
| **Approved — Pipeline** | Score 55–74; queued for next available capacity | Hold in prioritized backlog |
| **Deferred** | Score 40–54; revisit in 90 days | Return to sponsor with conditions |
| **Declined** | Score < 40 or disqualifying factors | Notify sponsor with rationale |

### Portfolio Balancing Criteria

The committee also considers the overall portfolio when making decisions:

- **Capacity balance** — No more than 40% of active initiatives in any single value domain
- **Horizon balance** — Maintain a mix of short-term (0–6 months), medium-term (6–18 months), and transformational (18+ months) initiatives
- **Risk balance** — Limit high-complexity/high-risk initiatives to 20% of active portfolio
- **Sponsor coverage** — Ensure adequate executive sponsor bandwidth across approved initiatives

---

## Stage 5: Initiation

Approved initiatives move into formal initiation, which includes:

1. **Project charter** — Scope, objectives, timeline, resources, and success criteria
2. **KPI baseline** — Documented current-state metrics for all selected KPIs
3. **Value commitment** — Sponsor-signed commitment to target outcomes
4. **Stakeholder alignment** — Kickoff with all key stakeholders
5. **Data access confirmation** — IT confirms data availability and access

Initiation must be completed within **30 days** of governance approval.

---

## Stage 6: Value Tracking

Each active initiative is expected to:

- Report progress monthly using the **[KPI Dashboard Template](../templates/kpi-dashboard-template.md)**
- Attend a **quarterly value review** with the Governance Committee
- Complete a **90-day post-launch review** documenting early results
- Complete a **12-month benefits realization review** confirming sustained value

See **[Governance](governance.md)** for the full review cadence and escalation paths.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Who can submit a use case?**
Any employee, leader, or authorized partner. All submissions require an identified executive sponsor.

**Q: How long does the process take from submission to decision?**
Typically 3–6 weeks for a complete submission: 5 days for screening, 10 days for evaluation, and one monthly governance cycle for decision.

**Q: Can a declined use case be resubmitted?**
Yes. Declined use cases may be resubmitted after 90 days if the circumstances have changed materially.

**Q: What happens to deferred use cases?**
Deferred use cases are reviewed again at the next quarterly governance meeting or earlier if conditions change.

**Q: Is there a limit on submissions per team?**
No limit on submissions, but sponsors should exercise judgment about readiness before submitting.
