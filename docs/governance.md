# Governance Model

> Decision rights, review cadence, escalation paths, and accountability structures for managing the AI and automation initiative portfolio.

---

## Purpose

The Governance Model establishes the structures and processes by which the organization makes decisions about its AI and automation portfolio. Strong governance ensures that:

- Investment decisions are made consistently and at the right level
- Accountability for value delivery is clear and enforced
- The portfolio is actively managed, not just approved
- Risks are visible and appropriately escalated
- Learning flows back into future decisions

---

## Governance Principles

1. **Decisiveness** — Governance structures are designed to make decisions, not defer them
2. **Proportionality** — Decision authority matches the scale and risk of the initiative
3. **Accountability** — Value commitments are tracked and reported; consequences exist for gaps
4. **Transparency** — Portfolio status, decisions, and rationale are visible to key stakeholders
5. **Adaptability** — Governance structures evolve as the program matures and the environment changes

## Governance Structure

### Governance Committee

The primary decision-making body for the AI and automation portfolio.

| Role | Member | Decision Authority |
|---|---|---|
| **Chair** | Chief Executive Officer or Chief Strategy Officer | Final approval authority; tie-breaking vote |
| **Clinical** | Chief Medical Officer | Clinical safety and quality veto authority |
| **Operations** | Chief Operating Officer | Operational feasibility and capacity |
| **Finance** | Chief Financial Officer | Financial value, ROI threshold, budget |
| **Digital/IT** | Chief Information Officer or Chief Digital Officer | Technical feasibility and architecture |
| **Workforce** | Chief People Officer | Workforce impact and change readiness |
| **Legal/Compliance** | Chief Compliance Officer or General Counsel | Risk, regulatory, and ethics |
| **Strategy/Innovation** | VP Strategy or Head of Innovation | Portfolio balance and strategic coherence |

**Quorum:** 5 of 8 members (must include Chair and at least one clinical representative)

**Meeting cadence:** Monthly decision meetings + quarterly portfolio reviews

---

### Operating Committee (PMO)

Manages the day-to-day pipeline: intake, evaluation coordination, and progress tracking.

| Role | Responsibilities |
|---|---|
| **Innovation/PMO Lead** | Pipeline management, committee coordination, reporting |
| **Program Managers** | Active initiative oversight, sponsor liaison |
| **Data/Analytics Lead** | KPI tracking, dashboard management, value measurement |
| **IT/Data Architecture Representative** | Technical feasibility assessment, data readiness review |
| **Compliance Representative** | Risk screening, regulatory check |

**Meeting cadence:** Weekly for pipeline management; daily during evaluation cycles

---

### Initiative Sponsor

Each approved initiative requires a named executive sponsor.

| Responsibility | Expectation |
|---|---|
| Champion the initiative | Visible advocacy to stakeholders and leadership |
| Commit to value | Sign value commitment statement at initiation |
| Remove blockers | Resolve escalated issues within defined SLA |
| Accountability | Present initiative status at quarterly portfolio reviews |
| Escalation | Raise issues requiring Governance Committee intervention |

## Decision Rights Matrix

| Decision | Sponsor | Operating Committee | Governance Committee |
|---|---|---|---|
| Submit a use case | **A** | I | I |
| Initial screening pass/hold/return | I | **A** | I |
| Full evaluation scoring | I | **R** | A |
| Approve / Defer / Decline | I | R | **A** |
| Assign resources and PM | R | **A** | I |
| Change initiative scope | **R** | A | I (unless Tier 1) |
| Pause or stop an initiative | R | **A** | I (unless Tier 1) |
| Adjust KPI targets | **R** | A | I |
| Adjust portfolio weights | I | R | **A** |
| Escalate patient safety concern | **A** | A | **A** |

*Key: A = Accountable (one owner), R = Responsible (executes), I = Informed*

## Review Cadence

### Monthly — Portfolio Decision Meeting

**Purpose:** Review scored use cases and make prioritization decisions

**Agenda:**
1. Recap of decisions from prior month (5 min)
2. New submissions ready for decision (30 min) — scored presentations
3. Deferred use cases for re-review (15 min)
4. Portfolio capacity check (10 min)
5. Escalations from active initiatives (15 min)
6. Decisions recorded and communicated (5 min)

**Inputs required:** Completed evaluation scorecards, capacity snapshot, escalation log

---

### Quarterly — Portfolio Review

**Purpose:** Review overall portfolio health, value realization, and strategic alignment

**Agenda:**
1. Portfolio dashboard review — all active initiatives (20 min)
2. Value realization report — initiatives ≥ 90 days post-launch (20 min)
3. At-risk initiatives — red/yellow status review (20 min)
4. Strategic alignment check — is the portfolio still the right portfolio? (15 min)
5. Governance model health — process, feedback, improvements (10 min)
6. Upcoming pipeline preview (15 min)

**Inputs required:** Portfolio dashboard, value realization report, at-risk log

---

### 90-Day Post-Launch Review

**Purpose:** Validate that an initiative is on track to deliver committed value

**Trigger:** Automatically scheduled 90 days after initiative launch

**Participants:** Sponsor, PM, analytics lead, relevant Operating Committee members

**Outputs:**
- Early outcome data vs. baseline
- Revised value forecast (if needed)
- Risk or blocker status
- Recommendation: continue / adjust / escalate

---

### 12-Month Benefits Realization Review

**Purpose:** Confirm that committed value has been realized and sustained

**Trigger:** Automatically scheduled 12 months after initiative launch

**Outputs:**
- Final KPI performance vs. targets
- Confirmed financial impact (validated by Finance)
- Lessons learned documentation
- Recommendation: sustain / expand / retire / document as case study

## Portfolio Status Definitions

Each active initiative carries a status that is reported at the monthly meeting and in the portfolio dashboard.

| Status | Definition | Required Action |
|---|---|---|
| **Green** | On track; value commitments being met; no significant blockers | None |
| **Yellow** | Minor risk or delay; value still achievable with intervention | Sponsor provides mitigation plan within 2 weeks |
| **Red** | Significant risk to value, timeline, or scope; intervention required | Escalate to Governance Committee; action plan due in 1 week |
| **On Hold** | Deliberately paused by sponsor or committee; restart date defined | Document reason; review at next governance meeting |
| **Complete** | Initiative concluded; value realization review scheduled | Trigger 12-month review; archive documentation |
| **Retired** | Initiative stopped before completion | Document rationale, lessons learned; update portfolio |

## Escalation Paths

### Tier 1 — Sponsor Resolution (SLA: 5 business days)
- Resource contention within the initiative team
- Minor scope clarification
- Access or permission blockers within the sponsor's control

### Tier 2 — Operating Committee Resolution (SLA: 10 business days)
- Cross-initiative resource conflicts
- Technical blockers requiring IT leadership involvement
- KPI baseline disputes

### Tier 3 — Governance Committee Resolution (SLA: Next scheduled meeting or emergency session)
- Budget changes > 20% of approved budget
- Scope changes that materially alter the value commitment
- Patient safety or compliance concerns
- Requests to pause or retire a Tier 1 initiative

### Emergency Protocol
For patient safety issues or critical compliance breaches, an emergency governance session may be called within 48 hours by the CMO, CCO, or CEO. The PMO lead is responsible for coordinating the session.

## Portfolio Reporting

### Monthly Portfolio Dashboard
Published within 5 business days after the monthly governance meeting. Includes:
- Initiative status (Green/Yellow/Red/On Hold/Complete/Retired)
- KPI performance vs. target for initiatives ≥ 90 days post-launch
- Pipeline summary (submissions, in evaluation, approved, deferred, declined)
- Capacity utilization

### Quarterly Value Report
Published within 10 business days after the quarterly review. Includes:
- Cumulative value realized to date (by domain and by initiative)
- Value on track vs. at-risk vs. confirmed realized
- Year-to-date portfolio summary for executive leadership
- Highlights and case studies

### Annual Portfolio Report
Published within 30 days of fiscal year end. Includes:
- Full-year value realization summary
- Portfolio performance against strategic objectives
- Governance effectiveness self-assessment
- Recommendations for the next fiscal year

## AI Ethics and Responsible AI Governance

All initiatives deploying AI models must comply with the organization's Responsible AI Policy. Governance-level checkpoints include:

| Checkpoint | Timing | Owner |
|---|---|---|
| Bias risk assessment | Before initiation approval | Compliance + Data team |
| Explainability review | Before go-live for clinical AI | CMO + CIO |
| Ongoing model monitoring | Quarterly for all live models | Data/IT team |
| Annual model audit | 12 months post-deployment | Compliance |
| Patient disclosure review | Before go-live for patient-facing AI | Legal + CMO |

Initiatives involving direct clinical decision support (Dimension 4 Risk score < 50) require additional clinical ethics review before Governance Committee approval.
