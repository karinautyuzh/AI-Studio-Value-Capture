Scoring Model

A weighted, five-dimension model that converts qualitative and quantitative assessment into a single 0–100 composite score for portfolio prioritization.

Dimensions & Weights

Five Scoring Dimensions

Each dimension is scored 0–100. Scores from multiple evaluators are averaged before weighting and calibrated in a committee session before governance decisions.

Composite Score Formula
Score = (Alignment × 0.25) + (Value × 0.30) + (Feasibility × 0.20) + (Risk × 0.15) + (Urgency × 0.10)

Decision Tiers

Prioritization Tiers

Tier 1 · Score 75–100
Approve: Priority
Proceed to initiation immediately. Assign PM and set charter timeline within 5 business days.
Tier 2 · Score 55–74
Approve: Pipeline
Queue for next available capacity. Hold in prioritized backlog; revisit when resources open.
Tier 3 · Score 40–54
Defer
Revisit in 90 days. Return to sponsor with written conditions for advancement.
Tier 4 · Score <40
Decline
Not recommended at this time. Notify sponsor with written rationale within 3 business days.
Automatic Disqualifiers
Regardless of composite score, a use case should not be approved if any of the following apply: active legal hold or litigation · unresolved patient safety risk identified in clinical review · regulatory prohibition blocking deployment · no identified executive sponsor willing to commit · duplicate of an initiative already in flight.
Customization

Weight Customization Guidance

Organizations may adjust dimension weights annually to reflect the current strategic context. Constraints: no dimension below 5%, no dimension above 40%, and weights must sum to 100%.

Strategic ContextSuggested Adjustment
Margin recovery program underwayIncrease Value Potential to 35%; reduce Urgency to 5%
Regulatory audit or accreditation cycleIncrease Risk to 25%; reduce Strategic Alignment to 15%
Competitive differentiation priorityIncrease Strategic Alignment to 35%; reduce Feasibility to 15%
Workforce crisis / high clinical turnoverApply 1.25× multiplier to Workforce domain KPIs in Value Potential sub-score
Patient safety incident recoveryIncrease Risk to 25%; apply veto authority to CMO on Risk score < 50
